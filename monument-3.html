German-Russian Museum Berlin-Karlshorst

Berlin-Karlshorst, Germany

The German-Russian Museum Berlin-Karlshorst is a World War II museum in Berlin located at the site where Nazi Germany signed its unconditional surrender to the Soviet Union on May 8, 1945. Formerly a military headquarters, the museum now focuses on the German-Soviet War (Eastern Front), presenting exhibits and artifacts that explain the causes, events, and consequences of one of the largest and deadliest conflicts of World War II.
